Votemos is a nonpartisan voter awareness platform created for Puerto Rico.

It’s designed with community in mind and hopes to bring Puerto Ricans

the tools they need in order to be ready for the 2020 elections.

created by: Mónica Nadal

Currently, the conversation around voting in Puerto Rico is stale. Elected Puerto Rican officials have a lot of power and their job is to bring solutions to our island. These elected officials form part of the dated system that does not represent the majority of Puerto Ricans. Their votes on local policies influence wages, rent, and our day to day lives.

But the voter, has even more power. Their vote can put someone into those seats and we need elected officials who understand our neighborhoods and can bring the change that our communities need. Now, before Puerto Ricans make their decision on who to vote for, they need to be informed on the importance of voting and how to register to vote.

Political climates change from election to election and it’s been five years since I lived in Puerto Rico. I got to experience the 2016 elections in Minnesota and before and after the election the conversations I was having with my friends, neighbors, and colleagues revolved around getting out the vote. This was an inspiring moment for me because I had never seen so much dedication from a community, coming together to have challenging conversations about who to vote for and the importance around voting.

My colleagues in particular were approached by a few organizations who had the intention to change the narrative around the 2017 Minneapolis elections. This eventually turned into the MPLS Voter Guide which brought together Voices for Racial Justice, Rhymesayers Entertainment, and Pollen to produce a voter guide for the City of Minneapolis elections to help people make the most inspired and informed decision before they cast their vote.
